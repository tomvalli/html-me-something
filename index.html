Bucket
List...
of Food

During this pandemic it has come to my attention that my bucket list partially consists of foods I have not eaten yet. There's certainly cool things I'd like to see and do but they seemed harder (and often too personal) to write about.

Sushi-rito

When I first heard of the sushi-rito (or sushiritto) it was something that I think was only available in the Pacific Northwest. I believe now you can get it in Saint Louis but I have yet to put in the effort to find it. When I read about this monstrosity I was relatively new to eating sushi and was (and still am) VERY excited at the prospect of turning those delicious little morsels of rice and fish and magic into a culinary affront to Jiro himself. Now that I think about it, a lot of the 'fusion' foods I make for myself mostly involve shoving some sort of Americanized foreign dish into a tortilla.

Pizza Roll Pizza

When I was younger I had an idea to combine two of the greatest foods on earth together into one unholy pie. I am now certain was probably only the millionth person to think of this but at the time I regarded myself as some sort of junk food Einstein. Think about it: You grab yourself a pizza shell, spread some sauce, plop on some ½ cooked pizza rolls, then cheese, then bake that bad boy. Strangely, I've worked in enough pizza places and kitchens that I have had ample opportunity to make this by now. I think I am scared that it won't be as good as I imagine and don't want to ruin it for myself (After a disheartening experience with Sloppy Joe pizza I'm not ready to spoil another dream quite so fast).

Mongolian Barbecue

After moving to Maplewood, MO from South Saint Louis City in 2018 I had to find a Chinese place to replace Bo Fung for my occasional General Tso's craving. It turns out I live incredibly close to the Joy Luck Buffet. Now, Chinese buffets (or any buffet really) can be extremely hit or miss but Joy Luck hits. They even have ice cream! We've only gone there a handful of times but the last time we went a table near us ordered some sort of huge platter covered in thinly sliced meats with a little burner in the middle. It looked amazing. I'm not even 100% sure that this was a Mongolian BBQ situation but it's the closest term I could find to describe it.

Eggs Benedict

Yeah, I know, you can get this like anywhere. For some reason when it comes time to order breakfast/brunch I just get all conservative. I mean, I've made hollandaise before as a line cook, I've just never eaten it on eggs. Someday I will.

Dim Sum

It's SO easy to find U.S.-Chinese food but I've never had "real" Chinese food. I can't say there's a specific dish I've always wanted to try but Chinese cuisine is super diverse and I think dim sum is the closest 'authentic' thing I can reasonably expect to find without having to travel very far.

Pho

I feel like the only person who's never had pho. I have no idea if I will like but I've liked other Vietnamese food I've tried and pho is so iconic I feel like I really need to give it a try.

Scotch Egg

It's a boiled egg (soft-boiled I think) wrapped in sausage, breaded, and fried. I like all those things. It's something I could almost certainly make at home, but after completing this bucket list I will likely die of heart disease so this will probably be my last meal. I should probably dine out for it ya know?
